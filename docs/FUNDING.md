# Funding Strategy -- Biodiversity Mapping Platform

## Does This Idea Have a Chance of Getting Funded?

Yes -- but not as a tech startup pitch.

It needs to be framed as **a biodiversity conservation project powered by citizen science.**

People donate to protect nature, support science, and help researchers -- not to fund apps. The messaging should emphasize:

- Protecting biodiversity in Mexico
- Helping scientists monitor species
- Empowering citizen scientists
- Supporting real research

**The app becomes the tool, not the product.**

---

## The Core Story (What People Donate To)

### The Problem

Mexico is one of the most biodiverse countries in the world. But many species are disappearing before scientists can even document them.

Researchers like Dr. Veronica Rosas, a professor of ecology at the University of Guadalajara, spend years studying wildlife -- amphibians, reptiles, and birds -- across western Mexico.

Yet there is a huge challenge: **scientists simply cannot be everywhere.**

There are thousands of nature enthusiasts encountering wildlife every day -- hikers, students, birdwatchers -- but their observations rarely reach scientific databases. Valuable ecological information is being lost.

### The Solution

We are building a citizen science platform that allows anyone to contribute real biodiversity observations to scientific research.

With this platform:

- **Researchers** can launch biodiversity monitoring projects
- **Citizen scientists** can document wildlife observations with AI-verified identification
- **Communities** can help protect local ecosystems

Every observation helps researchers understand how species populations are changing. Together, we can build one of the largest biodiversity monitoring networks in Mexico.

## 3-Year Hosting Budget

### The Biggest Cost: Photo Storage

Citizen science apps are image-heavy. Each observation includes 1-3 photos, GPS metadata, timestamps, and species identification data.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Average photo size (compressed) | 2-3 MB |
| Average per observation (2 images) | ~5 MB |

### Projected Growth

| Year | Observations | Cumulative Storage |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Year 1 | 5,000 | 25 GB |
| Year 2 | 25,000 | 150 GB |
| Year 3 | 100,000 | 650 GB |

With backups and redundancy: **plan for ~1 TB storage.**

### Cost Breakdown (3 Years)

| Component | Monthly | 3-Year Total |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Object storage** (photos, 1 TB) | $20-30 | $720 -- $1,100 |
| **Database** (Postgres, 50-100 GB) | $25-80 | $900 -- $2,800 |
| **Server / backend** (serverless) | $30-100 | $1,080 -- $3,600 |
| **Maps** (Mapbox or free Leaflet) | $0-100 | $0 -- $3,600 |
| **CDN and bandwidth** | $20-80 | $720 -- $2,800 |
| **AI species recognition** (API) | $20-100 | $720 -- $3,600 |

### Summary Budget Tiers

| Tier | 3-Year Hosting Cost | Description |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Lean research MVP** | $4,000 -- $5,000 | Small team, basic infrastructure |
| **Healthy research platform** | $8,000 -- $12,000 | Multiple institutions, active community |
| **Large community platform** | $15,000+ | Thousands of contributors, full analytics |

### Recommended Grant / Crowdfunding Budget

| Line Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Platform development | $15,000 |
| Hosting (3 years) | $10,000 |
| Community outreach | $5,000 |
| Field research support | $5,000 |
| **Total** | **$35,000** |

This makes the campaign credible and realistic. Hosting scales gradually -- you do not need large infrastructure on day one.
